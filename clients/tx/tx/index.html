Section C1 – Functional Interpretation

C1.1 Voltage / Frequency Support

Objective:

Ensure grid stability through standardized frequency–watt and volt–VAR control, following IEEE 1547-2018 / UL 1741 SB / Rule 21 requirements.

Frequency–Watt Control

  • Relationship: ΔP = −Kf × (f − f_nom)
  • Deadband: 59.8 – 60.2 Hz
  • Droop Kf: 3 – 5 % per Hz
  • Response Time: < 500 ms

Volt–VAR Control

  • Curve: Four-segment (Vmin 0.95 pu → Qmax, deadband 0.98–1.02 pu, Vmax 1.05 pu → −Qmax)
  • Q limit: ±0.33 pu of rated apparent power (S)
  • Response Time: < 200 ms

Volt–Watt Limiting

  • Engages ≥ 1.06 pu voltage; reduces active power linearly to 50 % Pn at 1.10 pu.
  • Hysteresis disengage ≤ 1.04 pu.

Priority Logic

  • Voltage events → Reactive (Q) priority.
  • Frequency events → Active (P) priority.

C1.2 Reactive Power Control

Control Modes: PF mode / fixed Q mode / volt-VAR mode.

Constraint: P² + Q² ≤ S² (rated current envelope).

Transformer Interface: 12.47 kV Y-connected secondary; target voltage 0.95 – 1.05 pu.

Communication: Modbus-TCP or DNP3 via SCADA / EMS, update interval ≤ 2 s.

Protection Coordination: Q-priority time delay 2 – 5 s to avoid hunting with OLTC.

C1.3 Virtual Synchronous Machine (VSM)

Purpose:

Emulate synchronous-machine inertia and damping to enhance dynamic frequency stability.

Control Equation: 2H (dω/dt) = Pm − Pe − D (ω − ω₀)

Parameters:

  • Inertia constant H = 2 – 3 s
  • Damping factor D = moderate
  • Response Time < 500 ms
  • Mode: Grid-following with virtual inertia (enabled only when grid present)
  • Black start / islanding functions disabled to maintain anti-islanding compliance.

Protection Link: Loss-of-mains (RoCoF, V/Hz, phase jump) triggers trip ≤ 2 s.

Section C2 – Engineering Implications and Design Coordination

  • Voltage Reference Node: 12.47 kV collector bus (default) → coordinate with 69 kV POI OLTC control.
  • Transformer Impedance: Affects voltage response sensitivity; deadband alignment recommended.
  • Thermal Impact: Reactive operation increases losses / temperature; automatic derating enabled.
  • Harmonics: Maintain LCL filter tuning per IEEE 519.
  • Communication Latency: All measurements and control loops must share the same electrical node to avoid phase oscillation.

Attachment E – KB Integrated BESS (12 Units) – Engineering & Guarantee Plan

Project: OAK VOLT STORAGE (9.99 MW, ≥ 20 MWh @ Year-10 @ POI 69 kV)

1. Executive Summary

Configuration comprises twelve (12) KB integrated containers, each rated 1.2 MW / 2.064 MWh at 690 V AC.

Three containers connect to one 690/12.47 kV transformer (~ 4.15 MVA) and a dedicated 12.47 kV switchgear panel.

Four identical groups form the collector bus at 12.47 kV, which interfaces to the utility substation step-up (69 kV POI).

The plant export limit is 10 MW. By optimizing AC path efficiency and cell degradation control, the design achieves

≥ 20 MWh usable energy at Year-10 (POI basis) without adding a thirteenth unit.

2. Plant Sizing and Guarantee Basis

Installed Power = 12 × 1.2 MW = 14.4 MW

Operational Limit = 10.0 MW (set by EMS/PCS)

Initial Energy (E₀) = 12 × 2.064 = 24.768 MWh

Delivered energy at POI:

E₁₀ = E₀ × η_POI × SOH₁₀ ≥ 20.0 MWh

Composite efficiency η_POI ≈ 0.972 (PCS 98.5 % × Transformer 99.2 % × Cable 99.5 %).

Thus SOH₁₀ required = 20 / (24.768 × 0.972) ≈ 83 %.

LFP chemistry with ≤ 0.5 C rate and 80 % DoD typically retains 83–86 % capacity after 10 years,

confirming technical feasibility.

3. Hardware Architecture

Each group contains three KB containers feeding a Δ/Yn-grounded 690/12.47 kV transformer (~ 4.15 MVA) and one metal-clad switchgear panel.

Four groups tie to a 12.47 kV collector bus with main metering and protection, then step up to 69 kV at the utility substation.

Transformers impedance 6–7 %, X/R 8–10. Each 12.47 kV panel rated 25 kA bus, metal-clad, withdrawable breaker.

Grounding and arresters per IEEE Std 80 and C62.11.

4. Efficiency Optimization

  • Operate PCS within 50–85 % Pn for highest η_pcs.
  • Use premium low-loss core transformers (guaranteed 99.2 %).
  • Oversize MV conductors (750 kcmil Al main runs).
  • Minimize auxiliary consumption during tests.
  • Apply same-node PT/CT for RTE measurement accuracy.

5. Degradation Control Measures

  • Limit C-rate ≤ 0.5 C; SOC window 10–90 %.
  • Maintain cell inlet ≤ 30 °C typical (≤ 35 °C peak).
  • Idle SOC ≈ 50–60 % during stand-by.
  • Perform regular balancing and thermal checks.
  • Seasonal derate schedule to avoid thermal stress.

6. Augmentation Rider (Safeguard)

If mid-life testing indicates forecast SOH₁₀ < 83 %, Supplier will either refresh ≤ 5 % of modules or install micro-augmentation kits within the existing KB enclosures.

Cost is included in the performance guarantee price, subject to the agreed cycle profile (1 cycle/day, 80 % DoD, ≤ 35 °C).

7. Grid Support and Controls

Complies with IEEE 1547-2018 and UL 1741 SB.

  • Frequency-Watt: ±0.2 Hz deadband, Kf 3 %/Hz, response < 0.5 s.
  • Volt-VAR: ±0.33 pu range, deadband 0.98–1.02 pu, response < 0.2 s, reference 12.47 kV bus.
  • Volt-Watt: limit > 1.06 pu, full limit at 1.10 pu.
  • VSM mode: grid-following (H = 2–3 s), future upgrade to grid-forming/black-start possible.

    SCADA communication Modbus-TCP (default) / DNP3 (optional), 1–2 s refresh.

8. Protection and Testing

12.47 kV feeders protected by SEL-751A (50/51, 50N/51N, 27/59, 46/47, 81O/U).

Transformer optional 87T, 67, 24; anti-islanding trip ≤ 2 s.

FAT and SAT include frequency/voltage response, VSM disturbance, P/Q priority, and communication latency tests.

9. Bill of Materials (Summary)

  • 12 KB integrated containers (1.2 MW / 2.064 MWh each).
  • 4 transformers (≈ 4.15 MVA, 690 Δ/12.47 kV Yn).
  • 4 metal-clad 12.47 kV switchgear panels.
  • Arresters, CT/VT, relays, SCADA RTU, LV/MV cabling, earthing materials.
  • UL / ITC documentation package and complete test records.

10. Commercial Guarantee Clauses

  • Energy Guarantee: ≥ 20.0 MWh delivered at POI (69 kV) in Year-10.
  • Efficiency Basis: η_POI ≥ 0.972 (PCS ≥ 98.5 %, Transformer ≥ 99.2 %, Cable ≥ 99.5 %).
  • Augmentation Rider: mid-life capacity correction at Supplier cost if forecast shortfall.
  • Operating Envelope: P ≤ 10 MW, C-rate ≤ 0.5 C, SOC 10–90 %.

1. Scope of Supply

This quotation covers one complete PowerStation™ 1200/2064 Integrated BESS module rated

1,200 kW / 2,064 kWh @ 690 V AC, combining battery, PCS, HVAC, fire-suppression, and auxiliary systems inside a single 20 ft container.

The overall project requires twelve (12) identical units to reach the contractual 10 MW / ≥ 20 MWh (Year-10) performance target.

Each container is factory-tested and UL9540/UL9540A certified.

For commercial evaluation, the prices shown below represent one set only.

Total project value = quoted unit price × 12.

Transformers and 12.47 kV switchgear are not included in this quotation; they may be supplied as third-party items upon client confirmation.

5. System Deployment Overview

Twelve containers form four groups (3 per transformer).

Each group connects to a 12.47 kV collector bus via its dedicated switchgear panel.

The collector bus feeds the utility step-up transformer to 69 kV POI.

All equipment is UL listed and FAT tested as complete systems.

[3×KB Units]──690 V Bus──[Δ/Yn 4.15 MVA TR]──[12.47 kV Panel]──Collector Bus──→ Utility Substation (69 kV POI)

6. Performance Alignment

When installed in twelve-unit configuration under Attachment E design, the system delivers:

  • Export Power Limit: 10 MW (aggregate)
  • Initial Usable Energy: 24.8 MWh @ 25 °C
  • Guaranteed Year-10 Energy @ POI: ≥ 20.0 MWh
  • Round-Trip Efficiency (RTE): ≥ 95 % @ 25 °C
  • Grid Functions: Volt-VAR, Freq-Watt, Volt-Watt, VSM (grid-following)
  • Compliance: IEEE 1547-2018, UL 1741 SB, NFPA 855, CPUC Rule 21.
